Project Details

Inventory Control Management

Built a full inventory-performance toolkit: flagged risky SKUs, quantified excess holding cost, simulated demand, and optimised reorder points & economic order quantities—turning 50 K $ of dead stock into actionable cash.

ABC Analysis EOQ / ROP Safety Stock Python Scripts Tableau

Business Problem

The company stocked hundreds of SKUs without a structured policy. Some items ran out (risking lost sales and customer trust) while many others sat dormant, tying up capital and warehousing cost. Management needed clear-cut triggers for when to reorder and how much to buy.

Approach

  • Data prep &feature engineering — fixed dtypes, merged lead-time fields, created DoS, annual sales value, holding-cost metrics.
  • ABC segmentation — flagged top 20 % SKUs (“A”) generating 80 % of revenue.
  • Risk & cost quantification — computed opportunity vs. excess-holding cost per SKU.
  • Policy optimisation — used Poisson variance for safety stock, derived reorder points, calculated EOQ at $300/PO, 20 % holding rate.

Key Results

  • $50 K of excess stock pinpointed in B/C items.
  • 0 stock-out opportunity cost: data confirmed over- not under-stocking.
  • EOQs 3–10 × larger than reorder points revealed a mis-set $300 ordering-cost assumption.
  • Simulated a 10–15 % inventory reduction by right-sizing ordering cost and service levels.

Recommendations

  • Raise reorder points on A-class SKUs whose DoS < lead time.
  • Freeze new orders on C-items with DoS > 90 days; clear stock via markdowns / kits.
  • Audit real ordering cost (likely $100-150) → smaller EOQ → 10 % lower inventory.
  • Target service levels: 95 % (A) / 90 % (B) / 85 % (C); recalibrate safety stock.
  • Stabilise lead times: no SKU above its class’s 75th-percentile lead time.
