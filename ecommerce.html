Project Details

E-Commerce Seasonal & Geographic Trends

Explored how seasonality and customer geolocation influence order-cancellation rates for a global sports-retail dataset (180 K+ orders, 2016 – 2018). Merged open-data, engineered seasonal features by hemisphere, and benchmarked statistical & machine-learning models to pinpoint drivers of lost revenue.

ANOVA Logistic Regression Robust OLS K-Means Clustering Seasonality Geolocation

Business Problem

High cancellation rates erode margins and distort inventory planning. Industry belief links cancellations to seasonal demand swings and the logistical challenge of serving dispersed global customers—but evidence for this link in sports e-commerce was limited.

Approach

  • Extracted season from order-date & hemisphere; created 4-season label.
  • Described cancellation ratios by 23 global regions and 4 seasons.
  • Ran two-way ANOVA to test joint effects; built heteroscedastic-robust OLS, logistic regression & K-means (k = 3) for deeper pattern mining.
  • Validated assumptions (normality, variance homogeneity, multicollinearity) and pruned leverage outliers.

Key Results

  • Seasonality not significant in any model (p > 0.80).
  • Region explains 46 % of cancellation variance (R² of OLS); highest risk: Canada; lowest: Central Asia.
  • Logistic model odds: Central Africa ↘ 1.3 ×, West Africa ↘ 1.2 × vs baseline.
  • K-means flagged 1 high-risk cluster (avg cancel > 11%), guiding targeted CX fixes.

Tech & Tools

Python · pandas · scikit-learn · statsmodels · Tableau · LA Open Data API
