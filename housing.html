Project Details

Housing Foreclosures & Median Income in Los Angeles

Investigated whether lower-income ZIP codes in LA County experience more residential foreclosures. Combined open-data foreclosure counts with demographic and housing-price variables, then applied robust regression to isolate income effects while controlling for population, prices, unemployment and poverty rates.

Robust Regression Housing Economics Socio-economics LA Open Data Outlier Analysis

Business Problem

Policymakers assume low-income neighborhoods face higher foreclosure risk, yet LA County shows unique price and leverage dynamics. Understanding the true income-foreclosure link informs targeted housing support and zoning decisions.

Approach

  • Compiled foreclosure counts (2014 – 2022) and median-income by ZIP from LA Open Data.
  • Augmented with housing prices, population, unemployment and poverty rates.
  • Cleaned missing ZIP records and removed extreme outliers based on leverage tests.
  • Ran heteroscedasticity-robust OLS and interaction-term models; validated with outlier-pruned sample.

Key Results

  • Higher-income ZIPs showed more foreclosures, contrary to national trend.
  • Suggests leverage & ownership rates, not raw income, drive LA foreclosure risk.
  • After removing outliers, the interaction of high prices × high population lost significance.

Tech & Tools

Python · pandas · statsmodels · matplotlib · LA Open Data API
